Marie Skłodowska Curie

The mother of modern physics and chemistry

A Timeline of Marie Curie's Life:

  • 1867: Born Maria Skłodowska in Warsaw, Poland.
  • 1891: Moves to Paris to study at the Sorbonne.
  • 1895: Marries French physicist Pierre Curie.
  • 1898: Marie and Pierre announce the discovery of polonium and radium.
  • 1903: Marie and Pierre Curie, along with Henri Becquerel, are awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for their work on radioactivity. Marie becomes the first woman to win a Nobel Prize.
  • 1906: Pierre Curie dies in a street accident. Marie takes over his professorship at the Sorbonne, becoming the first woman to hold such a position.
  • 1911: Marie Curie wins her second Nobel Prize, this time in Chemistry, for her discovery of radium and polonium and her isolation of pure radium. She is the first person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.
  • 1914-1918: During World War I, she develops mobile X-ray units, known as "petites Curies," to help wounded soldiers.
  • 1921: Visits the United States to raise funds for radium research.
  • 1934: Dies from aplastic anemia, likely caused by prolonged exposure to radiation.

"Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood. Now is the time to understand more, so that we may fear less."
